Lead claimant identifier

  • “It’s got the lead claimant on now! This is so important to have because you can’t give info to someone who isn’t the ‘lead’ claimant”
  • Would still prefer name of partner displayed as well as NINO
  • Gives confidence from a security perspective – prevents giving info to the other partner by mistake

Migration Notice - issued to one claimant, partner calls and is unable to attend

  • Found the process easy to go through
  • Wouldn’t issue Migration Notice until had spoken to claimant who is unable to attend, then would post individual notices on the same day. “I wouldn’t issue a migration notice if the partner isn’t coming in”
  • If different work coaches, would rely on communication between coaches to ensure all actions taken correctly.
  • “You’d have to work with the partner’s work coach”

Couples UI exploration

Scenarios

1. Log initial conversation for both claimants - same day
2. Log readiness conversation outcome - same day
3. Log Migration Notice issued
4. Log reminder issued
5. Give more time
6. Cancel Migration Notice

UR Feedback

  • Work coaches prefer to treat couples as single journeys because they are individuals
  • Especially important if the claimants have different work coaches and for security purposes
  • Confirmation of main claimant meaning - usually person who is paid
  • There is, however, a need to easily identify a partner through the system - comparisons to current LMS and UC ‘switch’ function. This also tested well in the prototype
  • To ensure the journey is linear, prompts on the success page to then populate partner details were well liked - it makes the journey easier. Our users really liked it and it helped them though this process. It made it easier for them, which is a key user need.
  • Cancelling a journey – there is an expectation that cancelling one person out of the journey would automatically cancel the other (check feasibility with Paul Brennan – he mentioned it was better from a data POV to keep them separate if possible). Cancelling a journey – sensitive information cannot be displayed on the partner record (e.g. cancelled due to partner reasons vs showing their partner went in to prison). We need further research on this
  • Making a claim – this is where the claim becomes one UC claim (one cannot be submitted without another)
  • If the partners broke up after he migration notice – when they go back in to the journey as a single claim, they would like to see the history that it was a previous couple claim. This will help work coaches provide better support to the claimant as they know their background
  • Lead claimant/partner terminology. There is a need to understand who the main claimant is, as they are the ones who get paid
